Investment Thesis
Key Findings
01 — Revenue
Service Revenue Tripled
Service revenue grew from $75B (2020) to $218B (2025) — a 3× increase. Service mix is now 77% of total vs 52% in 2020. Azure, Microsoft 365, and Dynamics compound at double-digit rates with high switching costs and predictable renewal streams.
5yr Service CAGR: 23.8%
02 — Margins
Operating Leverage Is Real
Operating margin expanded 900bps (37% → 46%) while gross margin held stable at ~69%. Each incremental dollar of service revenue requires minimal additional cost — textbook operating leverage driven by a fixed-cost infrastructure base supporting exponential demand.
Op margin: 37.0% → 45.6%
03 — Cash
FCF Machine Returns Capital
FCF grew from $45B to $72B despite $65B in FY2025 CapEx for AI/datacenter buildout. Shareholders received $42B in dividends and buybacks in FY2025 alone. The dividend has increased every year and the payout is well-covered at ~34% of FCF.
5yr FCF CAGR: 13.5%
04 — Balance Sheet
Fortress Finances
Total assets doubled to $619B. Debt/equity fell from 1.55× to 0.80× as the company self-finances AI infrastructure with operating cash flows. Interest coverage of 54× signals near-zero default risk. Microsoft is one of only two AAA-rated U.S. companies.
D/E: 1.55x → 0.80x
05 — AI Moat
R&D Investment Compounds
R&D held at 12–13% of revenue every year, reaching $32B in FY2025. The OpenAI partnership, Copilot integration across all products, and Azure AI platform create structural advantages in enterprise software that are years ahead of most competitors.
FY2025 R&D: $32.5B
06 — Risks
Watch These Closely
Key risks: (1) Azure growth deceleration as comparables get harder. (2) AI CapEx ROI uncertainty — $65B/yr is significant. (3) Regulatory scrutiny post-Activision. (4) FX headwinds on ~50% international revenue. (5) Current ratio declining toward 1×.
FY2025 CapEx: $64.6B (+45% YoY)
